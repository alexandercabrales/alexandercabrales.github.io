Current Career Goal

    My most important career goal at the moment is getting an entry-level job in the Information Technologies sector. As I've said before, I intially went to school to study our Criminal Justice system in order to become a police officer. However, after learning a lot about this system and the monster it has become I knew that I could not become a police officer. Ethically, yes, I could become a police officer but, morally, no, I could not start a career in law enforcement. Yet, instead of switching majors so close to a degree, I decided to finish my Bachelors. From here my next task was to pay off debt from school loans by working. After paying off the loans I was faced with a decision: keep working at In-N-Out and look long-term with working to get my own store, or save up some money, quit the job, and go back to school full-time. After thinking for about a month I came to the decision that I was missing something at In-N-Out and I didn't have a passion for the business, so I put in my two weeks, left on good terms, and I enrolled for Spring 2017 term at Ohlone College for Computer Science. However, after a rough semester, with a W for a late start Trigonometry class and a realization that my arithemetic and problem solving skills were lacking in the Math department. I thought I could cram Math to learn it faster for my Trigonometry and object-oriented programming class for Fall 2017. But I realized after the first day of the object-oriented programming that I didn't need to rush the process and that I need to take it a little slower or else I'm going to fail. So, I retook the Trigonometry class again, dropped the object-oriented programming class, added a computer introduction class, and got a job as a English tutor at Ohlone, which led me to being exposed to Python, 3D-printing, HTML, CSS, and the power of computers in our society (economically and technologically);also, I've been able to pursue my passion with helping others and English into an achievement with a CRLA certification and the building of Cabrales Critique (still being worked on). Now I feel that I'm on the cusp of a long and fullfiling IT career with a entry-level IT job. I feel that I have the skills and knowledge necessary to sort IT problems, assess obstacles, plan solutions and standards, and then look to sustain said solutions and standards. I'm currently getting my A.S. in Information Technologies with an Emphasis in Databases this Fall 2018, and I'm currently seeking any entry-level IT positions in which the company feels that I will be an asset to their organization and the company feels that I bring a passion for Information Technologies.
